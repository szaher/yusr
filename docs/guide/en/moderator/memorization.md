# Memorization Plans & Reviews

Create and manage student memorization plans, log reviews, and track Quran progress.

## Understanding Memorization Plans

Memorization plans provide structured progression through the Quran with automatic pace calculation and homework suggestions.

### Plan Components

**Starting Position:**
- Surah, verse, or page where student begins
- Can start anywhere in the Quran
- Set based on student's current memorization

**Pace:**
- How much to memorize per session
- Based on templates or custom settings
- Adjustable based on performance

**Template:**
- Predefined pace configurations
- Common: 1/4 Hizb, 1/2 Hizb, 1 Hizb, custom
- Determines pages per session

## Creating Memorization Plans

### Setting Up a Student Plan

To create a plan for a student:

1. Navigate to **Memorization**
2. Select a student or click **Create Plan** from student profile
3. Fill in plan details:
   - **Student** — Select student
   - **Starting Position** — Where to begin
     - Choose Surah and starting verse
     - Or select starting page number
     - Or continue from last review position
   - **Pace Template** — Select template:
     - **1/4 Hizb** — ~1.5 pages per session (8 years to complete)
     - **1/2 Hizb** — ~3 pages per session (4 years to complete)
     - **1 Hizb** — ~6 pages per session (2 years to complete)
     - **Custom** — Define your own pace
   - **Custom Pace** (if custom selected):
     - Pages per session
     - Or verses per session
4. Click **Create Plan**

### Pace Templates Explained

**1/4 Hizb Template:**
- Amount: Quarter of a Hizb (~1.5 pages)
- Sessions per Juz: ~40 sessions
- Total time: ~8 years (weekly sessions)
- Best for: Beginners, young students, part-time students

**1/2 Hizb Template:**
- Amount: Half a Hizb (~3 pages)
- Sessions per Juz: ~20 sessions
- Total time: ~4 years (weekly sessions)
- Best for: Intermediate students, consistent learners

**1 Hizb Template:**
- Amount: Full Hizb (~6 pages)
- Sessions per Juz: ~10 sessions
- Total time: ~2 years (weekly sessions)
- Best for: Advanced students, dedicated memorizers

**1.5 Pages Custom:**
- Common custom setting
- Flexible, easy to manage
- Similar to 1/4 Hizb

### Plan Activation

Once created:
- Plan is active immediately
- Next homework range is calculated automatically
- Student can view their plan and upcoming work
- Progress tracking begins

## Logging Memorization Reviews

### Recording a Review

After a student recites in session:

1. Navigate to **Memorization** > **Log Review**
2. Or click **Log Review** from student profile
3. Fill in review details:
   - **Student** — Select student
   - **Quran Range** — What they recited
     - Select Surah and verse range
     - Or enter page range
     - Or use **Next Range** (auto-suggested)
   - **Grade** — Overall score (0-100)
   - **Recitation Result** — Excellent, Very Good, Good, Needs Improvement
   - **Review Type** — New Memorization, Revision, Exam
4. **Tajweed Scores** (optional but recommended):
   - Score each Tajweed category (0-100)
   - Categories:
     - Makharij (pronunciation)
     - Sifaat (letter characteristics)
     - Ahkam (Tajweed rules)
     - Mad (elongation)
     - Ghunnah (nasalization)
     - Qalqalah (echo/vibration)
5. **Mistakes** (optional):
   - Record specific errors
   - Mistake type (omission, substitution, pronunciation, Tajweed)
   - Verse/word location
   - Notes for correction
6. **Notes** — Additional observations, strengths, areas to focus
7. Click **Save Review**

### Auto-Suggested Next Range

**computeNextRange Feature:**

The system automatically suggests the next homework range based on:
- Student's current position in their plan
- Pace template settings
- Previous progress
- Group meeting cadence

**Using Auto-Suggestion:**
1. When logging review, look for **Next Range** button or field
2. System displays suggested range (e.g., "Surah Al-Baqarah: 11-20")
3. Click to auto-fill
4. Adjust if needed
5. Saves time and ensures consistency

**Manual Override:**
- You can always override the suggestion
- Useful for:
  - Repeating a range (if student struggled)
  - Skipping to revision
  - Adjusting based on session length

## Setting Pace Overrides

### Temporary Pace Adjustments

Override the standard pace for specific situations:

**Next Session Only:**
- One-time pace adjustment
- Useful for:
  - Shorter session time
  - Student requesting extra challenge
  - Exam preparation
  - Holiday schedules

**How to Set:**
1. When logging review or creating assignment
2. Select **Override Pace**
3. Choose **Next Session Only**
4. Specify custom amount (pages or verses)
5. Save
6. Pace returns to normal after this session

**From Now On:**
- Permanent pace change
- Updates the memorization plan template
- All future homework calculated with new pace

**How to Set:**
1. Open student's memorization plan
2. Click **Adjust Pace**
3. Select **From Now On**
4. Choose new template or custom pace
5. Confirm change
6. All future ranges updated automatically

**When to Adjust Pace:**
- Student consistently excelling → Increase pace
- Student struggling → Decrease pace
- Life circumstances change
- Different learning season (Ramadan, summer)
- Approaching major milestone

## Pace Calculation Examples

### How Next Range is Calculated

**Example 1: Weekly Sessions, 1/4 Hizb Pace**
- Current position: Surah Al-Baqarah, verse 1
- Pace: 1/4 Hizb = ~1.5 pages = ~10 verses
- Next range: Verses 1-10
- Following session: Verses 11-20

**Example 2: Custom 2 Pages**
- Current position: Page 10
- Pace: 2 pages
- Next range: Pages 11-12
- Following session: Pages 13-14

**Example 3: Twice Weekly, 1/2 Hizb**
- Meeting cadence: Twice per week
- Pace: 1/2 Hizb = ~3 pages
- Session 1: Pages 1-3
- Session 2 (same week): Pages 4-6

## Tajweed Evaluation

### Detailed Tajweed Scoring

Provide comprehensive Tajweed feedback:

**Scoring Each Category:**
- **100** — Perfect application, no errors
- **90-99** — Excellent, very minor issues
- **80-89** — Good, some corrections needed
- **70-79** — Acceptable, notable errors
- **60-69** — Weak, significant issues
- **<60** — Poor, requires focused study

**Category Descriptions:**

**Makharij (Pronunciation Points):**
- Correct letter pronunciation from proper articulation points
- Common issues: Throat letters, tongue placement

**Sifaat (Letter Characteristics):**
- Proper expression of letter qualities (heaviness, lightness, etc.)

**Ahkam (Tajweed Rules):**
- Noon sakinah and tanween rules (Izhar, Idgham, Iqlab, Ikhfa)
- Meem sakinah rules

**Mad (Elongation):**
- Correct duration of elongation (2, 4, 5, or 6 counts)

**Ghunnah (Nasalization):**
- Proper nasal sound with Noon and Meem

**Qalqalah (Echo/Vibration):**
- Bouncing sound for specific letters (ق ط ب ج د)

### Tajweed Notes

Add specific Tajweed feedback:
- "Excellent Makharij overall, but watch Qaf vs. Kaf distinction"
- "Practice Mad Munfasil (2 counts only)"
- "Review Idgham rule for Noon followed by Ya"

## Revision vs. New Memorization

### Review Types

**New Memorization:**
- Student learning new material
- First time memorizing this portion
- Progresses their memorization plan
- Updates current position

**Revision:**
- Reviewing previously memorized material
- Maintaining retention
- Does not progress plan position
- Important for long-term retention

**Exam:**
- Formal assessment
- May cover new or revised material
- Typically broader range

### Logging Revision

When logging a revision review:

1. Select **Review Type: Revision**
2. Choose range being revised (can be any previous portion)
3. Grade based on retention and fluency
4. Note: Position in plan doesn't advance
5. Helps track overall retention

**Revision Best Practices:**
- Regular revision prevents forgetting
- Review older portions periodically
- Assign revision homework between new memorization
- Track revision separately from new progress

## Plan Management Tips

### Effective Plan Administration

**Regular Monitoring:**
- Review plan progress weekly
- Adjust pace if needed
- Ensure consistent progression
- Address gaps or struggles early

**Student Communication:**
- Explain the plan to students
- Set clear expectations
- Discuss pace adjustments
- Celebrate milestones

**Flexibility:**
- Plans are guides, not rigid rules
- Adapt to individual student needs
- Consider life circumstances
- Quality over speed

**Documentation:**
- Log every review
- Detailed notes help long-term
- Track Tajweed improvement
- Record mistake patterns

# Managing Groups

View, configure, and manage your assigned memorization groups (halaqat).

## Viewing Your Groups

Navigate to **Groups** to see all groups assigned to you.

### Group List

Each group displays:
- **Group Name** — Level, class, and group identifier
- **Student Count** — Number of enrolled students
- **Schedule** — Meeting day and time
- **Meeting Cadence** — Frequency (weekly, biweekly, etc.)
- **Status** — Active, on break, completed

### Group Details

Click any group to view full details:

**Group Information**
- Level and class assignment
- Group number/name
- Creation date
- Status

**Schedule**
- Meeting day
- Meeting time (in your timezone)
- Duration
- Meeting link template

**Students**
- Complete student list
- Student status (active, inactive)
- Quick access to student profiles

**Statistics**
- Total students
- Average attendance rate
- Average grade
- Average progress percentage
- Total sessions held

## Group Configuration

### Memorization Plan Settings

Enable or disable memorization plans for the entire group:

1. Open group details
2. Navigate to **Settings** tab
3. Toggle **Memorization Plans Enabled**
   - **ON** — Students follow structured memorization plans
   - **OFF** — Free-form memorization tracking
4. Click **Save Settings**

**When to Disable:**
- Students are at different levels
- Focusing on revision only
- Special review periods
- Exam preparation periods

**When to Enable:**
- Structured curriculum
- New memorization phase
- Consistent group level
- Standard progression

### Meeting Cadence

Set how often your group meets:

**Cadence Options:**
- **Weekly** — Once per week (most common)
- **Biweekly** — Every two weeks
- **Twice Weekly** — Two sessions per week
- **Custom** — Define specific schedule

**Setting Cadence:**
1. Open group settings
2. Select **Meeting Cadence**
3. Choose frequency
4. For custom: specify days and times
5. Save changes

**Impact of Cadence:**
- Affects pace recommendations
- Determines assignment schedules
- Influences attendance expectations
- Used for progress projections

### Meeting Day/Time

Configure default meeting schedule:

1. Navigate to group settings
2. Set **Default Meeting Day**
3. Set **Default Meeting Time**
4. Choose timezone (usually yours)
5. Set **Duration** (typically 60-90 minutes)

> **Note:** This sets defaults for new sessions. Individual sessions can override these times.

### Meeting Link

**Default Meeting Link:**
- Set a recurring virtual classroom link
- Applied to all new sessions automatically
- Can be overridden per session

**Supported Platforms:**
- Zoom
- Google Meet
- Microsoft Teams
- Other video conferencing URLs

**Setting Meeting Link:**
1. Open group settings
2. Enter **Default Meeting Link**
3. Test link validity
4. Save

### Adding Students

**Note:** Students are typically assigned by administrators. If you need to add a student:

1. Contact academy administration
2. Request student assignment to your group
3. Admin will transfer student
4. You'll receive notification

### Removing Students

To remove a student from your group:

1. Open student profile
2. Click **Actions** > **Remove from Group**
3. Provide reason
4. Confirm removal
5. Admin will review and process

> **Important:** Removing a student doesn't delete their account or data. It unassigns them from your group. Contact admin for account deactivation.

## Group Communication

### Group Announcements

Post announcements to your entire group:

1. Navigate to **Announcements**
2. Click **New Announcement**
3. Fill in details:
   - **Title** — Brief headline
   - **Message** — Full announcement text
   - **Priority** — Normal or Important
   - **Target** — Select specific groups or all your groups
   - **Attachments** — Optional files
4. Click **Publish**

**Announcement Uses:**
- Schedule changes
- Exam reminders
- Group-specific information
- Encouragement and motivation
- Upcoming events

### Group Messages (if enabled)

Some academies allow group messaging:

**Sending Group Message:**
1. Navigate to **Messages**
2. Select **New Group Message**
3. Choose recipient group(s)
4. Compose message
5. Send

**Message Etiquette:**
- Keep professional
- Avoid excessive messages
- Use announcements for important info
- Respect student privacy

# Exams

Take assessments to demonstrate your Quran knowledge, memorization, and understanding.

## Exam Overview

Exams in Yusr Academy evaluate your:
- Quran memorization accuracy
- Tajweed application
- Understanding of Quran content
- Islamic knowledge
- Progress and readiness for advancement

### Exam Types

**Memorization Exams**
- Recite specific portions
- Test retention and fluency
- Graded on accuracy and Tajweed

**Knowledge Exams**
- Multiple choice questions
- True/false questions
- Short answer questions
- Test understanding of Quran content and Islamic studies

**Comprehensive Exams**
- Combination of recitation and knowledge
- Cover larger portions
- Used for level advancement

**Progress Checkpoints**
- Periodic assessments
- Track learning trajectory
- Identify areas needing reinforcement

## Taking an Exam

### Starting the Exam

To begin an exam:

1. Click **Start Exam** on the exam details page
2. Review instructions one final time
3. Confirm you're ready
4. Click **Begin**
5. Timer starts (if time-limited)

> **Important:** Once started, you must complete the exam. Closing the browser or losing connection may use an attempt.

### Question Types

#### Multiple Choice

**Format:**
- Question stem
- 3-5 answer options
- Select one correct answer

**Example:**
```
In which Surah is the verse of the Throne (Ayat al-Kursi)?
○ Surah Al-Fatiha
○ Surah Al-Baqarah ✓
○ Surah Al-Imran
○ Surah An-Nisa
```

**Tips:**
- Read all options before selecting
- Eliminate obviously wrong answers
- Look for key words in the question
- Don't second-guess too much

#### True/False

**Format:**
- Statement
- Select True or False

**Example:**
```
Surah Al-Fatiha has 7 verses.
○ True ✓
○ False
```

**Tips:**
- Look for absolute words (always, never) — often false
- Consider each part of compound statements
- If any part is false, the whole statement is false

#### Short Answer

**Format:**
- Question requiring brief written response
- Text input field
- Word/character limit

**Example:**
```
Question: What is the meaning of "Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Raheem"?

Answer: [In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful]
```

**Tips:**
- Be concise and direct
- Answer exactly what's asked
- Check spelling
- Stay within word limit

#### Recitation Questions

**Format:**
- Request to recite specific verses
- Record audio or live recitation
- Graded on accuracy and Tajweed

**Process:**
1. Read recitation instructions
2. Review the verses (if open-book)
3. Click **Start Recording** or **Begin Recitation**
4. Recite clearly
5. Click **Stop** when finished
6. Review recording (if applicable)
7. Submit or re-record (if allowed)

**Tips:**
- Take a moment to prepare
- Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- Apply proper Tajweed
- Don't rush — accuracy over speed
- Check recording quality before submitting

## During the Exam

### Navigation

**Progress Indicators:**
- Question counter (e.g., "Question 5 of 20")
- Progress bar
- Answered vs. unanswered questions
- Flagged questions

**Navigation Buttons:**
- **Previous** — Go back to previous question
- **Next** — Move to next question
- **Flag** — Mark question for review
- **Review** — See all questions overview
- **Submit** — Finish and submit exam

### Time Management

**Timer Display:**
- Countdown timer (if time-limited)
- Warning when 10 minutes remain
- Warning when 5 minutes remain
- Auto-submit when time expires

**Time Strategies:**
- Note total time and question count
- Allocate time per question
- Answer easy questions first
- Flag difficult questions for later review
- Save 5-10 minutes for final review

### Saving Progress

**Auto-Save:**
- Answers saved automatically every 30 seconds
- Can safely close and resume (if settings allow)
- Progress preserved during connection issues

**Manual Save:**
- Click **Save Progress** to save immediately
- Useful before taking a break (if permitted)

### Reviewing Answers

Before submitting:

1. Click **Review Answers**
2. See overview of all questions
3. Indicators show:
   - ✓ Answered
   - ✗ Unanswered
   - 🚩 Flagged for review
4. Click any question to jump to it
5. Make changes as needed

## Submitting the Exam

### Final Submission

To submit your completed exam:

1. Answer all required questions
2. Review flagged questions
3. Click **Submit Exam**
4. Confirm submission (this action is final)
5. View confirmation screen
6. Receive submission receipt

> **Warning:** Submission is final. You cannot change answers after submitting (unless retake is allowed).

### What Happens Next

**Auto-Graded Questions:**
- Multiple choice, true/false: Graded immediately
- Instant score for these questions

**Manually Graded Questions:**
- Short answer, recitation: Graded by moderator
- Notification when grading is complete
- May take 1-7 days

## Retaking Exams

### When Retakes Are Allowed

Retakes may be permitted when:
- Exam settings allow multiple attempts
- You didn't pass (score below passing threshold)
- Moderator grants additional attempt
- Technical issues occurred

### Attempt Limits

**Common Limits:**
- **1 Attempt** — No retakes (high-stakes exams)
- **2 Attempts** — One retake allowed
- **3 Attempts** — Two retakes
- **Unlimited** — Practice exams

**Attempt Tracking:**
- Shows "Attempt 1 of 2" during exam
- Previous attempt scores visible
- Best score or most recent score counts (check exam settings)

### Retaking Process

To retake an exam:

1. Review your previous attempt results
2. Study areas where you scored low
3. Navigate to the exam
4. Click **Retake Exam**
5. Confirm you want to use another attempt
6. Begin the new attempt

**Retake Settings:**
- Questions may be different (from question pool)
- Order may be shuffled
- Same time limit applies
- Previous answers not shown

> **Study Tip:** Don't retake immediately. Review your mistakes, study the material, and retake when you're better prepared.

## Exam Preparation Tips

### Study Strategies

**Before the Exam:**
1. Review all coverage material thoroughly
2. Practice recitation of exam range
3. Test yourself with practice questions
4. Review previous session feedback
5. Get adequate rest the night before
6. Ensure technical setup is ready

**During the Exam:**
1. Read instructions carefully
2. Manage your time wisely
3. Answer easier questions first
4. Flag difficult questions for review
5. Double-check answers before submitting

**For Recitation Exams:**
1. Practice in a quiet environment
2. Record yourself and listen back
3. Focus on Tajweed accuracy
4. Recite at a moderate, clear pace
5. Review the verses one final time

### Technical Preparation

**Before Exam Day:**
- Test your internet connection
- Check microphone quality
- Verify browser compatibility
- Clear browser cache
- Close unnecessary programs
- Charge device fully

**During the Exam:**
- Use wired internet if possible
- Close other browser tabs
- Disable notifications
- Have backup device ready
- Keep moderator contact info handy

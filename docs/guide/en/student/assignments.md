# Assignments

View, complete, and track your Quran study assignments including memorization, revision, Tajweed practice, and homework.

## Assignment Types

Yusr Academy supports four types of assignments:

### Quran Memorization

**New memorization assignments**
- Specific Quran range to memorize
- Due date for recitation to moderator
- Preparation guidelines
- Estimated completion time

**Typical Content:**
- Surah and verse range (e.g., "Surah Al-Baqarah: 1-10")
- Page numbers
- Number of verses
- Tajweed focus points

### Quran Revision

**Review assignments for previously memorized portions**
- Consolidate older memorization
- Prevent forgetting
- Strengthen retention
- Identify weak areas

**Typical Content:**
- Range to review
- Specific focus areas
- Expected fluency level
- Self-assessment checklist

### Tajweed Practice

**Focused recitation quality improvement**
- Specific Tajweed rules to practice
- Audio resources
- Practice exercises
- Recording requirements (optional)

**Common Tajweed Topics:**
- Makharij (letter pronunciation points)
- Mad (elongation rules)
- Ghunnah (nasalization)
- Qalqalah (echo/vibration)
- Idgham, Ikhfa, Iqlab, Izhar (nun sakinah rules)

### Homework

**General Islamic studies and Quran-related work**
- Reading assignments
- Research tasks
- Reflection exercises
- Written responses
- Video/audio content

**Examples:**
- Read Tafsir for a specific surah
- Watch lecture on Tajweed
- Complete worksheet on Quran themes
- Write reflection on ayah meanings

## Completing Assignments

### Memorization Assignments

To complete a memorization assignment:

1. Review the assigned Quran range
2. Memorize according to your method
3. Practice recitation until fluent
4. Self-check for accuracy
5. Click **Mark as Prepared**
6. Optional: Add preparation notes
7. Recite to moderator in next session

**Preparation Tips:**
- Break range into smaller chunks
- Repeat each verse 10-20 times
- Practice without looking
- Record yourself and listen back
- Review before sleeping

> **Note:** Marking as prepared notifies your moderator that you're ready to recite. This doesn't complete the assignment — completion happens after successful recitation.

### Revision Assignments

To complete a revision assignment:

1. Review the specified range
2. Recite without looking
3. Note any weak spots
4. Practice problem areas
5. Self-assess fluency
6. Click **Mark as Reviewed**
7. Recite to moderator if required

**Self-Assessment Questions:**
- Can I recite fluently without hesitation?
- Am I applying correct Tajweed?
- Have I corrected previous mistakes?
- Is my pronunciation accurate?

### Tajweed Practice Assignments

To complete a Tajweed assignment:

1. Review Tajweed rule instructions
2. Listen to provided audio examples
3. Practice the specified verses focusing on the rule
4. Record yourself (if required)
5. Submit recording or confirm practice
6. Mark as complete

**Practice Methods:**
- Listen to expert recitation
- Slow recitation focusing on rules
- Repeat difficult words/phrases
- Compare your recitation to model
- Use Tajweed color-coded Mushaf

### Homework Assignments

To complete homework:

1. Read/watch/complete required materials
2. Complete any worksheets or exercises
3. Upload submission if required:
   - Click **Upload File**
   - Select file (PDF, DOC, image, audio, video)
   - Add comments if needed
   - Submit
4. Or click **Confirm Completion** for listening/reading assignments

**Supported File Types:**
- Documents: PDF, DOC, DOCX, TXT
- Images: JPG, PNG
- Audio: MP3, M4A, WAV
- Video: MP4, MOV (size limits apply)

## Confirming Listening Completion

For audio/video assignments:

1. Listen to or watch the entire content
2. Take notes or reflect
3. Click **Confirm Listening Completion**
4. Optional: Add reflection notes
5. Answer any comprehension questions

> **Honor System:** Confirmation indicates you've completed the assignment honestly. Your learning depends on genuine engagement.

## Assignment Status Tracking

### Status Indicators

**Not Started**
- Assignment created but no action taken
- Appears in pending list
- No preparation logged

**In Progress**
- You've started working on it
- May have uploaded partial work
- Marked as "preparing"

**Pending Review**
- Submitted and awaiting moderator review
- For homework/recording submissions
- Cannot edit while under review

**Complete**
- Moderator confirmed completion
- May include grade or feedback
- Moves to completed assignments list

**Overdue**
- Past due date
- Highlighted in red
- May affect grade
- Still can be completed

### Status Timeline

2. Started — [Date] when you first viewed it
3. Prepared — [Date] when marked as ready
4. Submitted — [Date] if submission required
5. Reviewed — [Date] by moderator
6. Completed — [Date] final status

## Requesting Extensions

If you need more time:

1. Open the assignment
2. Click **Request Extension**
3. Provide reason:
   - Personal circumstances
   - Difficulty level
   - Scheduling conflicts
   - Health issues
4. Suggest new due date
5. Submit request

**Extension Approval:**
- Moderator reviews request
- Approval/denial notification sent
- Due date updated if approved
- Original due date noted in history

> **Best Practice:** Request extensions at least 2 days before the due date when possible.

## Late Submissions

**Late Submission Policy:**
- Assignments can be completed after due date
- May receive grade deduction (moderator discretion)
- Shows as "Late" in submission history
- Better late than never — always complete your work

**Impact of Late Work:**
- May affect completion rate statistics
- Could impact progress to next level
- Affects learning and retention
- Communicate with moderator about challenges

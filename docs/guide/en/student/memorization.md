# Memorization Progress

Track your Quran memorization journey with comprehensive progress tracking, review history, and detailed analytics.

## Your Memorization Plan

### Viewing Your Plan

Navigate to **Memorization** from the sidebar to view:

1. **Current Position** — Your current location in the Quran
   - Juz number (1-30)
   - Hizb within the juz (1-2)
   - Quarter within the hizb (1-4)
   - Page number (1-604)
   - Surah name and verse

2. **Next Homework Range** — What you should prepare for your next session
   - Starting point (surah, verse, page)
   - Ending point
   - Number of pages/verses
   - Expected completion date

3. **Memorization Pace** — Your learning speed
   - Pages per week
   - Current pace template (e.g., "1/4 Hizb" or "1.5 Pages")
   - Estimated completion date

### Understanding Pace Templates

Your moderator assigns a pace template based on your ability:

| Template | Amount per Session | Pages per Week | Completion Time |
|----------|-------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| 1/4 Hizb | ~1.5 pages | ~1.5 pages | ~8 years |
| 1/2 Hizb | ~3 pages | ~3 pages | ~4 years |
| 1 Hizb | ~6 pages | ~6 pages | ~2 years |
| Custom | Varies | Varies | Varies |

> **Note:** Pace can be adjusted based on your progress and performance. Talk to your moderator if you feel the pace is too fast or slow.

## Review History

### Complete Review Log

Your review history table shows all memorization checks logged by your moderator:

| Date | Range | Grade | Result | Mistakes | Tajweed | Moderator |
|------|-------|-------|--------|----------|---------|-----------|
| May 30 | Surah Al-Baqarah: 1-10 | 95 | Excellent | 1 minor | 92/100 | Ustadh Ahmad |
| May 27 | Surah Al-Baqarah: 11-20 | 88 | Good | 2 mistakes | 85/100 | Ustadh Ahmad |

**Table Columns:**

1. **Date** — When the review took place
2. **Range** — Quran portion reviewed (surah, verses, or pages)
3. **Grade** — Overall score (0-100)
4. **Result** — Performance assessment
   - Excellent (90-100)
   - Very Good (80-89)
   - Good (70-79)
   - Needs Improvement (<70)
5. **Mistakes** — Number and type of errors
6. **Tajweed Score** — Recitation quality (0-100)
7. **Moderator** — Who conducted the review

### Review Result Meanings

**Excellent (90-100)**
- Minimal or no mistakes
- Strong Tajweed application
- Confident recitation
- Ready to move forward

**Very Good (80-89)**
- Few minor mistakes
- Good Tajweed
- Some hesitation acceptable
- Can proceed with review

**Good (70-79)**
- Several mistakes requiring correction
- Tajweed needs attention
- Additional review recommended
- May need to repeat this portion

**Needs Improvement (<70)**
- Significant mistakes
- Tajweed concerns
- Must review and repeat
- May affect pace progression

## Tajweed Scores

### Understanding Tajweed Assessment

Your moderator evaluates Tajweed across categories:

**Common Tajweed Categories:**
- **Makharij (مخارج الحروف)** — Letter pronunciation points
- **Sifaat (صفات الحروف)** — Letter characteristics
- **Ahkam (أحكام التجويد)** — Tajweed rules
- **Mad (المد)** — Elongation rules
- **Ghunnah (الغنة)** — Nasalization
- **Qalqalah (القلقلة)** — Echo/vibration

**Per-Category Scores:**
Each category is scored separately, giving you specific feedback on where to focus your improvement efforts.

### Tajweed Progress Tracking

View your Tajweed trends:

- **Overall Tajweed Average** — Across all reviews
- **Category Breakdown** — Performance per Tajweed rule
- **Improvement Trends** — Chart showing progress over time
- **Common Mistakes** — Recurring Tajweed errors to address

> **Study Tip:** Focus on one Tajweed category at a time. Master it before moving to the next.

## Mistakes Log

### Learning from Errors

Each review records mistakes to help you improve:

**Mistake Types:**
- **Word Omission** — Skipped word
- **Word Addition** — Added extra word
- **Word Substitution** — Wrong word used
- **Pronunciation Error** — Incorrect pronunciation
- **Tajweed Error** — Tajweed rule violation
- **Hesitation** — Long pause or uncertainty

**Mistake Details:**
- Exact location (surah, verse, word)
- Mistake type
- Frequency (if recurring)
- Correction notes from moderator

### Improvement Recommendations

Based on your mistake patterns, the system may suggest:

- **Specific verses to review**
- **Tajweed rules to study**
- **Audio resources for difficult sections**
- **Practice exercises**

## Memorization Tips

### Effective Memorization Strategies

**Daily Routine:**
1. Review previous memorization before starting new portions
2. Break new portions into small chunks (3-5 verses)
3. Repeat each verse 10-20 times
4. Recite full passage without looking
5. Review before sleeping

**Retention Techniques:**
- Understand the meaning (read translation)
- Listen to professional recitation
- Identify patterns and repetition
- Connect verses to themes
- Regular revision schedule

**Quality Over Quantity:**
- Focus on accuracy and Tajweed
- Don't rush to move forward
- Ensure strong foundation before advancing
- It's better to memorize less with excellence than more with mistakes
